# Wingman — Dispatcher Action Sets by Delay Bracket

For every bracket the model assigns to a flight, the dispatcher has a defined set of actions to take. Maximum four actions per bracket — kept tight on purpose so the operator can execute immediately.

---

## Bracket 0 — On time (0–15 min)
**Color: Green**

1. Monitor

---

## Bracket 1 — Minor delay (15–60 min)
**Color: Yellow**

1. Notify gate crew to expedite turnaround
2. Pre-position fuel, catering, and baggage
3. Check next leg for cascade risk
4. Log in dispatcher feed

---

## Bracket 2 — Moderate delay (60–120 min)
**Color: Orange**

1. Extend turnaround buffer in schedule
2. Alert standby crew (do not activate yet)
3. Notify connecting flight desks
4. Verify crew duty time remaining

---

## Bracket 3 — Major delay (120–180 min)
**Color: Red**

1. Activate standby crew
2. Distribute meal vouchers (EU261 Article 9)
3. Initiate aircraft swap evaluation
4. Rebook at-risk connecting passengers

---

## Bracket 4 — Severe delay (180–300 min)
**Color: Dark Red**

1. Execute aircraft swap
2. Process EU261 Article 7 compensation
3. Book passenger hotels immediately
4. Replace crew if duty limit approached

---

## Bracket 5 — Cancellation zone (300+ min)
**Color: Black**

1. Cancel the flight
2. Rebook passengers onto alternate flights
3. Cover hotels, meals, transport
4. Release crew and reposition aircrafts
